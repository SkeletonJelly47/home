Mikko Peltola, Game Programmer

I'm a game programming graduate from Kajaani University of Applied Sciences currently working on Drive Ahead! at Dodreams.

Programming passions

As a programmer my biggest interest is making gameplay mechanics that feel good moment to moment. First project where I felt that we achieved good game feel was Dashbot Ninja. We dedicated time from out schedule to 'add juice' to the game for the first time. In Ron Colt, my job was to make the player animations feel good and responsible. This includes animation canceling, scaling animation speed with attack speed intervals and still keeping the combo re-activation consistent. I must do this while still keeping compatibility with all different animations with relative ease.

My current passion in programming is learning test driven development and behaviour driven development. It's something I haven't seen too widely adopted in game development, and I believe that it could be something that pushes the envelope of software quality in games. In pursuit of knowledge, I enrolled in Dave Farley's TDD & BDD - Design Through Testing course. Now I'm keeping up the practice with simple coding katas with cyber-dojo.

Game development summer intensive course at KUAS, Teacher, 2018 & 2019

I ran a one week intensive course on game development with Unity, with the goal of students making a small finished game. The course consisted of 2 days of instructed lecturing where the students learned the basics of Unity and C# programming. The rest of the course, students developed a small game either following an example project that I instructed live or they could develop their own idea, depending on their existing know-how. At the end of the course, every student completed a small game and did a small presentation on how they did it. The course attendees had some international students so all of the lecturing was in english.

Me as a programmer

My best skill as a programmer is definitely creating systems to match needs of a game / designer. I feel that I work most efficiently when we've conceived a plan with the programming team, based on the requirements of the designer and aim to match those requirements as efficiently as possible. When I have a goal to work towards I can get there.

All of that requires good scoping from the programmers and designers which I make sure to be weary of. I always try to keep things as simple as possible to avoid over engineering and to eliminate unnecessary early optimizations. My second best skill is communicating complex systems to other team members, whether or not they are a programmer.

I have dabbled in sound design in my school game projects, particularly in Selene. I have interest for creating sound effects and sound scapes. For Dashbot Ninja I created most of the sound effects and co-composed the music with another team member.
